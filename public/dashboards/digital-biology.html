The theme is real. The clean basket is not.

Digital biology — AI, automation and biology converging on molecule and protein design — is inflecting for reasons that hold up to scrutiny. But "digital biology" is not a coherent thing to buy. Public exposure splits into three profiles that behave nothing alike, and the purest plays carry the most binary risk while the names with real cash and moats are only fractionally exposed. 01

Buy the theme through three different risk profiles, not one ticker

The convergence is genuine and the capital following it is large. What breaks is the instinct to treat it as a single trade. Once you segment by what actually pays you back, the universe separates into infrastructure that earns regardless of which drug works, growth platforms betting on a compounding data advantage, and clinical-stage names that are option value on binary events. Each behaves differently in a drawdown, prices off different signals, and rewards a different holding period.

The most investable, best-capitalised names are only partially exposed to the theme — a large-cap toolmaker or compute vendor gets a rounding-error of revenue from digital biology. The purest plays are small, mostly pre-revenue, and swing on trial readouts. That trade-off — how much theme-purity is worth paying for — is the organising question here, not a footnote.

02

The inflection is a capability shift, not just a narrative

Three things changed at once. The science crossed a threshold, the first clinical proof point landed, and — most important for public investors — large pharma stopped buying assets and started buying capability.

The scientific base is now Nobel-validated: structure prediction and de-novo protein design moved from research curiosity to industrial input. A generative-AI-origin molecule then cleared a real efficacy bar for the first time. And the money followed a specific pattern — incumbents committing billions not for a single drug but for platforms, compute and data infrastructure. That is the signal that matters, because it is durable demand rather than a funding-round headline. The counter-signal is equally real: the last AI-drug hype cycle disappointed, and several early leaders were delisted or absorbed.

2020 → 2024
Structure & design go industrial. AlphaFold2, then AlphaFold3 extending to multi-molecule interactions; the 2024 Nobel in Chemistry for computational protein design. The inputs to biologics discovery become computable.
June 2025
First clinical proof point. An AI-discovered, AI-designed TNIK inhibitor posts a positive Phase 2a in idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, published in a leading medical journal — the first molecule with clinical proof-of-concept where both target and compound came from generative AI.
Jan → Feb 2026
Pharma buys capability; the IPO window cracks open. A wave of billion-dollar-plus capability deals (a $1B compute co-innovation lab; multi-billion platform and access agreements across large pharma). Two AI/biology pure-plays IPO within weeks — the largest biotech offerings since 2024.
Through 2026
Build phase, not pilot phase. Industry surveys show the majority of R&D organisations increasing AI budgets and rebuilding data pipelines and wet–dry lab integration — spending that flows to infrastructure and data, whichever drug programs ultimately win.

06

Four competing hypotheses, held open on purpose

Not a stacked bull case. These genuinely compete; the right portfolio depends on which one you believe. Confidence is my read of current evidence — each pairs with the specific thing that would move it.

H1

Shovels win

Infrastructure — sequencing, single-cell/spatial, DNA synthesis, compute — captures value regardless of which drug program succeeds. You sidestep binary clinical risk and ride category-wide spend.

ConfidenceModerate–high
What would update itTools revenue growth re-accelerating and attributable to AI customers would confirm it. The disconfirmer is live: if AI cuts experiments-per-program, consumable demand could fall — 10x's flat top line is the early tell to watch.
H2

Platform data-moats compound

As foundation models commoditise, the durable edge is proprietary data plus a tight wet–dry loop. A few integrated platforms take most of the value within their niches.

ConfidenceModerate
What would update itA heavily-owned platform asset reaching pivotal data or approval, or sustained high-margin data-licensing growth (Tempus is the cleanest live test). Against it: the best-validated platform (Isomorphic) isn't investable, and models keep leapfrogging.
H3

Pure-plays are option value

Most listed pure-plays are lottery tickets on binary events; a few re-rate hard on catalysts. The category's defining proof point so far sits at an offshore/private name, not a US-listed one.

ConfidenceHigh (as characterisation)
What would update itAn AI-origin drug clearing Phase 3 with clean data — or an improvement in Phase 2→3 conversion for AI-designed molecules — would upgrade the whole cohort from "option" to "franchise." Continued early-stage-only wins keep it as option value.
H4

Value accrues to incumbents

Cross-cutting bear case: the real winners are big pharma and Big Tech, who buy capability and in-license the best assets — squeezing listed pure-plays between commoditising models and pharma's own build-out.

ConfidenceModerate
What would update itA wave of pure-play acquisitions at premiums (Ajax→Lilly at $2.3B is a data point for equity holders) would refute the squeeze. Pharma internalising capability while pure-plays strand would confirm it.
07

What breaks the whole thesis

These apply across every profile above, and matter most for Profile C. None is a reason to dismiss the theme — each is a reason to size and stage exposure carefully.

Clinical base rates still govern

Roughly nine in ten clinical programs fail. AI has demonstrably compressed early-stage speed and cost — not yet late-stage success. Until an AI-origin drug clears Phase 3 and approval, the core value claim remains partly unproven.

Capital intensity & dilution

Wet-dry platforms and clinical pipelines burn cash for years. Several names fund themselves through at-the-market equity sales; "trades near cash" can become a value trap if burn outlives the catalyst.

Hype-cycle drawdown

The last AI-drug cycle produced delistings and forced mergers. Two 2026 IPOs are already down 20%+ from offer. Narrative can compress multiples fast when a marquee readout disappoints.

Macro & rate sensitivity

Long-duration, unprofitable biotech is acutely rate-sensitive, and academic/research end-markets swing with public funding. The theme's beta to financial conditions is high, independent of the science.

08

Thematic exposure is not the only game — and a real portfolio showed it

A concrete contrast worth holding in view. A basket of ABSI · ABCL · IOVA looks nothing like a digital-biology theme play — IOVA isn't even an AI name. It reads as a special-situations book: heavily de-rated single names, each with a balance-sheet cushion and a dated catalyst, several plausible acquisition targets. Same sector, opposite objective function.

Objective A · Thematic exposure

Own the convergence across the value chain

Optimises for durable exposure and investable quality — including profitable or near-profitable infrastructure (ILMN, NVDA, TEM, TWST) and the platform bellwethers (RXRX, SDGR). Lower single-name variance; you're betting the theme compounds over years.

This is what the six deep dives are built for.

Objective B · Special situations

Maximise asymmetry in beaten-down single names

Optimises for a re-rating on a specific event or takeout. Higher variance, more binary, driven by balance sheet and catalyst rather than the theme. Two of the three (ABCL, ABSI) happen to be AI-antibody; the third is a distressed cell-therapy commercial turnaround.

09

Where to point due diligence next — mapped to your bet

Not a buy list and not a single recommendation — the honest output is which names to diligence depends on which hypothesis you're backing. Start here, then verify against primary filings.

If you back H1 · Shovels

Infrastructure first

TEM · TWST · TXG
context: ILMN, NVDA

Diligence the durability of AI-customer demand and, for Twist, the breakeven timeline. Tempus is the highest-quality operating business of the group; Twist the cleanest pure shovel if it hits breakeven.

If you back H2 · Platforms

Data-moat provers

TEM · SDGR · RXRX

Test whether the data flywheel is real and monetising. Schrödinger's dual model is the most defensible; Recursion is the highest-variance expression of the same thesis. Watch model-commoditisation risk closely.

If you back H3 · Option value

Catalyst & cash coverage

ABCL · ABSI · GENB
event-driven, size small

Diligence the readout date, the cash runway through it, and the downside floor. ABCL is the standout on cash-vs-cap; treat these as staged, small, event-driven positions — not core holdings.

The one cross-cutting view I hold with reasonable confidence: separate the trade from the theme. Decide first whether you want durable thematic exposure (Objective A) or asymmetric single-name bets (Objective B) — mixing them without deciding is how the drawdowns catch you off guard.
