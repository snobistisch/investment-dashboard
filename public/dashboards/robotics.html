The structural fact most coverage misses: a standard 6-axis industrial robot uses ~4 RV reducers and ~2 harmonic reducers per unit, and reducers are the single largest cost block in the arm. Humanoids invert the mix — 28+ joints skew toward strain-wave gears, roller screws and micro-motors, which is why the demand shock lands on Stage B/C suppliers first, not on the robot brands. Three competing theses — and the evidence ledger for each

These were developed during research, not retrofitted. All three cannot be fully right at once; the disagreements between sources are shown, not smoothed over.

T1 — Component monopolies win regardless of which robot brand wins

Evidence balance: strong on demand, weak on price

Reducers, screws and magnets are oligopolies with decade-long manufacturing moats. Whoever wins the humanoid race buys from the same five joint factories. Buy the toll road, not the cars.

Supporting evidence

  • Leaderdrive monthly harmonic-reducer capacity ramped 50k (Q1-26) → ~70k → plan 100–120k units by end-2026; management: "orders filled through 2027" Morgan Stanley field research, Jun 2026; industry conference remarks Apr 2026 — ESTIMATE/REPORTED
  • Nabtesco + HDS estimated 50–55% of premium precision-reducer value; Nabtesco ~60% of RV reducers, >90% in medium/heavy-load segment historically DataIntelo 2026; ResearchInChina — ESTIMATE
  • HDS FY2025 (ended Mar-26): sales +7%, March-quarter orders at a 4-year high; FY2026 guide +14% sales HDS results / Morningstar, May 2026 — VERIFIED
  • Reported $685M Tesla linear-actuator order to Sanhua ≈ components for ~180,000 units, deliveries from Q1-2026 36Kr/Teslarati, Oct 2025 — REPORTED
  • Morgan Stanley raised China humanoid shipment forecast 28k → 50k units for 2026, citing supply-chain capacity checks and a ¥6.8B State Grid order MS note via BigGo, Jun 2026 — ESTIMATE

Contradicting evidence

  • HDS trades at ~143x P/E vs a modeled "fair" 40.6x and a 15.1x JP machinery average — and above the ¥4,419 analyst consensus target; Jefferies downgraded to Underperform into the rally Simply Wall St, May 2026; Investing.com — ESTIMATE
  • Leaderdrive trades ~44% above the average analyst target (¥488 vs ¥272 consensus); TTM revenue just $86M against an ~$11B market cap Investing.com / PitchBook, Jul 2026 — VERIFIED price data
  • Chinese domestic reducer challengers compress the duopoly's China pricing; Nabtesco's China RV share already slipping in AMR/cobot segments Jared Watkins Nabtesco research, Apr 2026 — ESTIMATE
  • Humanoid joint architecture may shift value from RV toward strain-wave + screws — good for some toll roads, bad for others; the "monopoly" is not one road Nabtesco analysis, Apr 2026 — ESTIMATE

Live disagreement

Sanhua's order: Chinese media and brokers reported a $685M / ¥5B Tesla actuator order in Oct 2025; Sanhua itself issued a clarification announcement calling the rumor "untrue" with nothing disclosable. Q1-2026 delivery timing later appeared in supply-chain reporting anyway. Either the order exists under NDA-shaped disclosure, or the single most-cited datapoint in the picks-and-shovels case is wrong. 36Kr Oct 2025 vs 36Kr Mar 2026 recap of Sanhua clarification

Nabtesco's share: figures of "~35%" (of all articulated joints) and "~60%" (of RV-class reducers) circulate simultaneously — different denominators, routinely conflated in bull cases.

T2 — The cost curve is Chinese; the West is a customer, not a competitor

Evidence balance: strong today, policy-fragile

Component economics already resemble solar and EV batteries: the Yangtze Delta cluster converts auto-parts know-how into robot joints at prices Japan and the West cannot match. Access is the trade — A-shares, HK listings, Stock Connect — not US tickers.

Supporting evidence

  • Chinese suppliers hold ~70% of Optimus V3 component share per securities-firm supply-chain research after factory audits BigGo synthesis of CN broker research, Apr 2026 — REPORTED
  • Chinese companies shipped ~87% of all humanoid robots globally in 2025; six of the top ten producers Chinese Omdia via TechMarketBriefs — ESTIMATE
  • IFR: China = 54% of global industrial robot installs (295k units, record) in 2024; domestic makers outsold foreign brands at home for the first time (57% share, up from ~28% a decade ago) IFR World Robotics 2025, Sep 2025 — VERIFIED (industry body)
  • Unitree: 2025 revenue ¥1.70B, adjusted net profit ¥591M, gross margin 60.1%, 5,500+ humanoids shipped — hardware profitability no Western humanoid firm has shown Unitree STAR prospectus / Caixin — VERIFIED
  • Magnets: China ~85–90% of NdFeB refining/production; each humanoid needs 2–4 kg; export-permit regime already operating Rare-earth industry estimates; permit reporting 2025 — ESTIMATE

Contradicting evidence

  • Tesla near-shoring is real: Sanhua, Tuopu, Xinjian, Beite, Xusheng approved for Thailand factories near-shoring to Giga Texas; AI5 chip moving to Samsung Texas; non-Chinese NdFeB chains being seeded Tom's Hardware Feb 2026 via supply-chain trackers — REPORTED
  • KR-Asia: China's 63% component share "rests on cost and efficiency" — erodible if design thresholds fall and production localizes KR-Asia, Jan 2026 — ESTIMATE
  • China's own planner (NDRC) warned of a robotics bubble and duplicative startups crowding out research NDRC via Futurism, Dec 2025 — VERIFIED statement
  • Pentagon listed Unitree among companies with alleged military ties (disputed by Unitree) — a preview of the sanctions tail-risk on the whole access trade TNW, Jul 2026 — REPORTED

Live disagreement

Who ships the most humanoids: Unitree's prospectus claims 32.4% of 2025 global unit share (#1); at least one Omdia estimate puts AgiBot ahead. Both cannot be right; unit-share claims in this sector are marketing artifacts until audited.

Keli Sensing: appears on Optimus torque-sensor supplier lists in broker notes, yet the company publicly denied technical cooperation with Tesla or humanoid joint-module firms (May 2023). Supplier-list inclusion ≠ purchase orders.

T3 — Humanoids are 3D-printing-2013 / lidar-SPACs-2021 with better demos

Evidence balance: strongest on valuation, weakest on demand direction

The pattern is familiar: a real technology, a genuine long-term market, and a 2–3 year window where equity prices assume the endgame arrives immediately. The trade is not "short robots" — it is refusing to pay narrative multiples for cyclical component earnings.

Supporting evidence (for the bear case)

  • 35 listed Korean robotics firms: combined 2025 sales ¥2.27T KRW, down 4.6% YoY — against a combined market cap of ~₩41.7T ≈ 18.4x sales. Rainbow Robotics: ~₩13T cap, 178 employees, Samsung halo Robot News via Seoul Economic Daily / GPT-EA synthesis, Apr 2026 — ESTIMATE
  • HDS at 143x P/E incl. a ¥4.8B one-off gain; JP and CN reducer stocks peaked the same day (26 May 2026) — synchronized global narrative chasing Simply Wall St; Kabu-chan analysis, Jun 2026 — ESTIMATE
  • Unitree's growth decelerated from +333% (2025) to +68% (Q1-26) with adjusted profit −52.6% on the eve of its IPO; company cites "industry enthusiasm gradually cooling" in its own prospectus SSE review filing, May 2026 — VERIFIED
  • Morningstar on Hengli Hydraulic: humanoid revenue "immaterial over the next five years," shares overvalued — after a 70% narrative-driven run Morningstar, Mar 2025 — ESTIMATE
  • Rodney Brooks (Roomba inventor): current humanoid investment largely wasted, robots unsafe for promised tasks; IEEE Spectrum: 2025 progress "hard to rationalize" against money raised; HBS's Andy Wu: humanoids "suboptimal for nearly every conceivable use case" absent smartphone-scale volume Futurism Dec 2025; IEEE Spectrum Jan 2026; Harvard Magazine Jun 2026 — expert opinion

Contradicting evidence

  • Unlike lidar SPACs, the picks-and-shovels layer is profitable today: Leaderdrive 2025 net profit +100%+ to ¥124M; Unitree GM 60.1%; Fanuc record FY2025 sales, OP +15.7% Company results 2025–26 — VERIFIED
  • Paying deployments exist: Figure×BMW Spartanburg ran 10-hour shifts on a live line across 30,000+ vehicles, expanding to Leipzig summer 2026; State Grid placed a ~¥6.8B order in China BMW/Figure case reporting Apr–May 2026; MS note — REPORTED/ESTIMATE
  • Unit costs fell ~40% 2023→2024 (Goldman via Deloitte); BofA models sub-$17k by 2030 — cost curves this steep rarely reverse Deloitte Tech Trends 2026 — ESTIMATE

Live disagreement

Same facts, opposite reads: bulls cite Leaderdrive's "orders full through 2027" as demand proof; bears note capacity-constrained orderbooks were exactly the 2021 lidar tell — bookings ≠ recurring demand, and Q1-2026 sell-side ratings on Leaderdrive itself split 9 buy / 3 sell with consensus targets 44% below price.

Historical analogy dispute: 3D printing 2013 had no profitable component layer; this cycle does. The analogy holds for integrator valuations, breaks for the transmission oligopoly. Track them separately.

05

Key risks — and what would falsify each thesis

A thesis without a falsification condition is a mood. These are the observable events that should force a re-rate of each view.

Optimus ramp slips (kills T1 pricing)

Musk's guidance of 50k–150k units in 2026 is the demand anchor under every reducer/screw multiple. Actuator orders sized for ~180k units are reported, not confirmed.

Falsifier for T1Tesla FY2026 delivers <20k Optimus units, or Sanhua/Tuopu robot-segment revenue fails to inflect in H1-2026 reports → toll-road multiples (100x+) are unsupported.

Export controls cut both ways (kills T2 access)

Rare-earth permits, the Pentagon list on Unitree, and possible US restrictions on A-share/ADR access could strand Western capital in the very names with the best unit economics.

Falsifier for T2Sanctions or delisting action against a major CN robotics supplier, or Thailand/US near-shored capacity reaching >30% of Optimus content by 2027 → the "China owns the chain" premium unwinds.

Deployments compound (kills T3)

The bear case dies quietly if boring metrics improve: robots per site, task hours per unit, repeat orders from the same customer.

Falsifier for T3Two or more non-automotive customers (logistics, retail, healthcare) publish repeat humanoid orders with disclosed uptime/ROI in 2026–27, or Korean listed-sector revenue swings from −4.6% to double-digit growth → the bubble frame is wrong.

Architecture risk (reshuffles Stage B/C)

Joint design is not settled: strain-wave vs planetary vs cycloidal vs direct-drive, roller screws vs ball screws, hands with 15+ DoF vs simple grippers. Each choice picks different winners.

Falsifier (component-level)A high-volume design (Optimus V3/V4, Figure 03+) dropping harmonic reducers for planetary or direct-drive joints in teardowns → HDS/Leaderdrive TAM assumptions break.

Narrative concentration

JP and CN reducer names topped on the same day (26 May 2026); Korean foreign inflows rotated ₩4T out of chips into robots in one week (4–8 May). Crowded factor, thin exits.

Signal to watchForeign-flow reversal on KRX robotics names and simultaneous multiple compression across JP/CN reducers would mark the theme trading as one global position — de-grossing risk, not fundamentals.

Data honesty risk (applies to this dashboard)

Much of the humanoid supply-chain "evidence" is broker channel checks amplified through Chinese financial media. Two named companies (Sanhua, Keli) have formally denied reports still cited as fact.

Standing ruleAny REPORTED-tier claim contradicted by a company announcement gets downgraded, not averaged. Denials are data.

Access routes for the geographic blind spots

Japan (TSE)

Direct via most international brokers (IBKR etc.). HDS also trades OTC (HSYDF) and on Frankfurt as K3R — same shares, thinner liquidity. Fanuc, Yaskawa, Nabtesco, THK, Keyence all liquid on TSE Prime.

China A-shares

Shanghai/Shenzhen names (Leaderdrive, Sanhua, Tuopu, Shuanghuan, Moons', JL MAG-A) via Northbound Stock Connect through HK brokers; STAR Market names need Connect eligibility or QFII funds. Unitree post-IPO likely accessible only after Connect inclusion, typically months later.

Hong Kong & ADRs

UBTech (9880.HK) and JL MAG H-shares (6680.HK) trade freely via HK. Hesai is a US ADR (HSAI) — simplest access, but carries US-China listing risk both ways.

Korea (KRX/KOSDAQ)

Rainbow Robotics and Doosan Robotics require KRX access (IBKR supports it). Foreign flows into these names hit records in May 2026 — liquidity improved, crowding did too.

ETF wrappers

BOTT (pure humanoid, heavy KR/CN, +114% 1-yr, thin AUM), KOID (Solactive humanoid index, low fee), BOTZ (broad, JP-heavy), ROBO (89 equal-weighted, most diversified, least humanoid). Wrapper choice ≈ thesis choice: BOTT expresses T1+T2, ROBO hedges toward T3.

Not directly investable

Figure ($39B Series C, Sep 2025), AgiBot, Boston Dynamics (inside Hyundai Motor — the "most under-priced robotics optionality in Korea" per KB Securities framing), Schunk, Maxon, Wittenstein (private German Mittelstand — a persistent gap in the listed universe).
